DisplayWord is designed around how real services run — built by someone who has sat at the operator console himself.

Three Screens

One operator.
Three independent displays.

The Hall, Stage, and Preacher screens each show exactly what they should — simultaneously. Your congregation sees clean lyrics. Musicians see chords. The preacher sees what's coming next.

  • Hall screen: lyrics for the congregation
  • Stage screen: chords, musician cues, key info
  • Preacher screen: coming slide, verse reference
  • Blackout button — removes all screens instantly
  • Works with any combination of monitors & projectors

Chords & Modulation

Every song in
any key, instantly.

Songs are stored with chords positioned above the lyrics. The worship leader decides the key — DisplayWord transposes everything in real time. Mid-song modulation is a single + or − button.

  • Chords displayed above each line of lyrics
  • One-click transposition to any key
  • Live modulation block: raise or lower key mid-song
  • Supports standard and Hebrew/RTL notation
  • Auto-detects key from chord set

Special Cues

Instant signals
to your musicians.

During worship the operator sends cues to the stage screen — A Cappella, Drums Only, Repeat, Quiet — as bright banners that appear for a few seconds. The congregation never sees them.

  • Pre-built cues with icons and colors
  • Custom cues: create your own with any icon, text, color
  • Two modes: brief banner or full-screen
  • Accent mode: louder, bolder, attention-grabbing
  • Separate cue sets for Worship and Sermon

Song Library

All your songs,
organized and ready.

Store your full song catalog with lyrics, chords, keys, and notes. Multiple song books for different communities or languages. Build a service setlist in minutes.

  • Multiple song books (communities, languages)
  • Full song editor with chord placement
  • Filter and search by title, key, vocalist
  • One-click setlist building — click to add, click again to remove
  • Print chord sheets as PDF for musicians
